## A Manifesto for Personal Context Sovereignty

### We believe:

A person is more than an account, an ID, or a dataset.

A person is the long-term accumulation of context:
- how they relate to others  
- how they express ideas and emotions  
- how they make decisions  
- how they understand the world  
- how their patterns evolve over time  

This context is not merely *data*.
It is an extension of human identity.

---

### We observe a growing risk:

As AI systems become agents — acting *for* people, not just responding *to* them — control over personal context increasingly shifts away from the individual.

- Platforms build opaque user models  
- Applications retain irreversible behavioral memory  
- AI systems “become you” without clear, revocable consent  

This creates a future where:
- Identity is inferred, not owned  
- Agency is assumed, not granted  
- Context is extracted, not entrusted  

**We reject this trajectory.**

---

### We assert a foundational principle:

> **Personal context should belong to the individual — not to platforms, models, or applications.**

AI systems should not *possess* who you are.  
They may only *temporarily operate within boundaries you define*.

---

### Our proposal:

Every person should be able to maintain a **Personal Context Vault**:

A user-owned, local-first system that:
- Stores personal context under the user’s control  
- Allows explicit, scoped, revocable authorization  
- Enables AI systems to *use* context without *extracting or copying it*  
- Prevents permanent ownership or opaque replication  

AI should interact with people through **context injection**, not data extraction.

---

### Why open source?

Any closed system that controls personal context inevitably becomes a new platform of power.

Therefore:
- Core abstractions must be open  
- Authorization models must be auditable  
- Control must remain with the user  

Open source is not a distribution strategy here —  
**it is a governance requirement.**

---

### This is not a product manifesto.

It is a boundary statement.

A declaration of what should *never* be centralized again.
