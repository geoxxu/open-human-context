# Governance

## Human Context Protocol (HCP)

---

## Purpose of Governance

Human Context Protocol (HCP) defines boundaries around how AI systems may interact with human context.

Because this protocol touches identity, agency, and personal sovereignty,  
**its governance must prioritize long-term trust over short-term efficiency.**

This document exists to ensure that:
- No single entity can control HCP
- No contributor can redefine its principles unilaterally
- No commercial interest can override human context sovereignty

---

## Core Governance Principles

### 1. Human Sovereignty First

Any evolution of HCP must preserve the principle that:

> **Human context belongs to the individual, not to platforms, models, or applications.**

No proposal that weakens user ownership, revocability, or explicit authorization
may be accepted, regardless of technical merit.

---

### 2. Protocol Neutrality

HCP is:
- Model-agnostic  
- Vendor-neutral  
- Platform-independent  

Governance decisions must not favor:
- Any specific AI provider
- Any commercial ecosystem
- Any deployment architecture (cloud or local)

---

### 3. Separation of Protocol and Products

HCP defines **rules and interfaces**, not implementations or products.

- Product features
- Business models
- User experiences

**must not be encoded into the protocol itself**.

This separation is non-negotiable.

---

### 4. Open, Auditable Decision-Making

All governance discussions, proposals, and decisions must be:
- Publicly documented
- Accessible to the community
- Traceable over time

No private agreements or closed-door decisions may redefine the protocol.

---

## Maintainers

### Role of Maintainers

Maintainers are **stewards**, not owners.

Their responsibilities are to:
- Preserve the core principles of HCP
- Review proposals for protocol alignment
- Facilitate consensus, not enforce authority
- Reject changes that violate sovereignty or agency boundaries

Maintainers **do not**:
- Own the protocol
- Control downstream implementations
- Grant exclusive rights or endorsements

---

### Maintainer Selection

- Initial maintainers are project initiators.
- Future maintainers may be added through community consensus.
- No single organization may control a majority of maintainer seats.

If a maintainer is employed by or affiliated with a commercial entity,
they must disclose that affiliation publicly.

---

### Maintainer Removal

A maintainer may be removed if they:
- Repeatedly violate core principles
- Act in undisclosed conflict of interest
- Attempt to centralize control over the protocol

Removal requires a public proposal and consensus among remaining maintainers.

---

## Contributions & Proposals

### Contribution Scope

HCP accepts contributions that:
- Clarify protocol boundaries
- Improve security or auditability
- Strengthen user control and revocability
- Improve interoperability without reducing sovereignty

HCP does **not** accept contributions that:
- Introduce hidden data extraction paths
- Encode commercial incentives
- Reduce transparency or auditability
- Shift ownership away from the user

---

### Proposal Process

Significant changes must follow a proposal process:

1. Public proposal document (e.g., HCP-PROPOSAL-XXXX)
2. Clear statement of intent and trade-offs
3. Explicit analysis of impact on user sovereignty
4. Open discussion period
5. Maintainer consensus decision

Silence does not imply consent.

---

## Forking & Compatibility

Forking is permitted and respected.

However:
- Forks must not present themselves as HCP-compliant unless they preserve core principles
- Compatibility claims must be explicit and verifiable
- Users must not be misled about protocol guarantees

HCP favors **compatibility over control**, but not at the cost of integrity.

---

## Commercial Use

Commercial implementations of HCP are allowed.

However:
- No implementation may claim ownership of the protocol
- No commercial terms may override protocol guarantees
- No branding may imply official endorsement without consensus approval

HCP exists to prevent lock-in — not to create new ones.

---

## Conflict Resolution

In cases of dispute:
- Human sovereignty takes precedence
- User agency overrides convenience
- Long-term trust outweighs short-term adoption

If consensus cannot be reached,
maintainers must favor **restrictive safety boundaries** over permissive expansion.

---

## Amendments to This Governance

This governance document may only be amended through:
- Public proposal
- Explicit justification
- Maintainer consensus

Any amendment that weakens user control, auditability, or revocability
must be rejected.

---

## Final Statement

Human Context Protocol is not governed for speed.

It is governed for **correctness over decades**.

Once a protocol defines how AI may interact with human identity,
**mistakes cannot be rolled back easily**.

This governance exists to ensure that HCP remains
a boundary — not a platform.

---

**Human Context Sovereignty is not negotiable.**
